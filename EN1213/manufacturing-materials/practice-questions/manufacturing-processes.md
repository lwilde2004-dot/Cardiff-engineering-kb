## Q1 — Turning / MRR — Easy

**Given:** A turning operation on a steel bar.  
Cutting speed V_c = 120 m/min, feed f = 0.25 mm/rev, depth of cut a_p = 2 mm.

**Find:** Material removal rate (MRR).

---

**Solution:**

$$\text{MRR} = V_c \times f \times a_p = 120{,}000 \times 0.25 \times 2 = \textbf{60{,}000 mm}^3\textbf{/min}$$

(Convert V_c to mm/min: 120 m/min × 1000 = 120,000 mm/min)

**Answer:** MRR = 60,000 mm³/min = 1000 mm³/s

## Q2 — Process Selection — Short Answer

**Given:** You need to machine a hardened steel component (65 HRC) with a complex internal profile.  
Conventional milling cutters cannot achieve the hardness.

**Find:** Which advanced machining process is most appropriate and why?

---

**Answer:**

**EDM (Electro-Discharge Machining)** is most appropriate because:
- Can machine **any conductive material regardless of hardness** — the material is removed by electrical discharges, not cutting forces
- Complex 3D internal cavities are achievable via **die-sinking EDM**
- No cutting forces → no distortion of the workpiece

Alternative for through-profiles: **Wire EDM** — a wire electrode cuts precise 2D profiles through the full thickness.

Waterjet would also work (no hardness limit) but cannot achieve the complex 3D internal profile.
