## Q1 — Crystal Structures — Easy

**Given:** Iron exists in two allotropic forms: α-Fe (below 912°C) and γ-Fe (above 912°C).

**Find:** (a) Name the crystal structures of each form. (b) Which is more ductile and why?

---

**Answer:**

**(a)**
- α-Fe: **BCC** (Body-Centred Cubic)
- γ-Fe: **FCC** (Face-Centred Cubic)

**(b)** γ-Fe (FCC) is more ductile because FCC has **12 slip systems** compared to BCC's fewer active slip systems at room temperature. More slip systems means more possible planes on which dislocations can move, allowing greater plastic deformation before fracture.

## Q2 — Phase Diagram / Lever Rule — Medium

**Given:** A binary eutectic system. At 200°C, the two phases present are:  
α phase with composition X_α = 10 wt% B  
β phase with composition X_β = 90 wt% B  
Overall alloy composition X_0 = 35 wt% B.

**Find:** Mass fraction of each phase.

---

**Solution:**

**Lever rule:**
$$f_\alpha = \frac{X_\beta - X_0}{X_\beta - X_\alpha} = \frac{90 - 35}{90 - 10} = \frac{55}{80} = \textbf{0.6875}$$

$$f_\beta = 1 - f_\alpha = 1 - 0.6875 = \textbf{0.3125}$$

**Answer:** f_α = 68.75% &ensp;|&ensp; f_β = 31.25%

*Check: (0.6875 × 10) + (0.3125 × 90) = 6.875 + 28.125 = 35 wt% ✓*
